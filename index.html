Stephen Klos

Web Designer and UI Engineer from Metro Detroit.

EDUCATION

I am an alumna from Michigan State University. I majored in Media and Information with a concentration in Social and Interactive Media and a Minor in Information Technology.
Yeah, I know... quite the mouthful.

Prior to transferring to MSU, I earned my Associate's of Science at Schoolcraft College.

EMPLOYMENT

I am currently employed at Perficient Digital as a UI Engineer. My team and I design and build dyanmic websites for a wide-array of clients. Depending on the scope of the project, my roles range from maintaining the front-end on a current site, to building all the UI (templates/components) from scratch, to collaborating directly with clients -- establishing optimized client-side solutions.

During my senior year of college I worked at Jackson National Life, a top-ranked US financial services company based in Lansing. During my time there, I assisted in the following: maintaining and updating websites for several of Jackson's affiliated companies, establishing customized style guides for specific projects, researching industry-related web standards, producing internal referencing documents, and creating wireframes/mock-ups for supplementary content.

Also while enrolled at MSU, I dipped my feet into a few other professional web development experiences. I got my first gig at SNworks, a partner company of The State News -- MSU's official newspaper. There, a handful of other students and myself designed and built responsive websites for newspapers from major universities (including MSU's). We built these sites around our in-house Content Management System called Gryphon. We maintained our sites (roughly 30) through Agile Workflow using a ticket-based system (JIRA).

AAAlife

Created at: Perficient

About

AAAlife is a life insurance company based in Livonia, Michigan. On their website one can research all of their life insurance policies to determine which one is right for you. Aside from static pages, the site contains web apps such as calculators, compare/contrast tools, and dynamic forms.

Roles

I had a number of roles on this project. Initially my role was building out responsive templates and components based on PSD’s. I built many of the dynamic components such as the accordions in the FAQ section. After the website launched my roles had changed. I led a team that was in charge of optimizing the performance of the site. My primary duty was to determine everything on the site that was slowing it down, then develop solutions to make aspects of the site perform better. This included, minifying and concatenating files, caching external requests, refactoring and caching JavaScript calls.
